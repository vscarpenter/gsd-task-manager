01 · The idea

The whole product in one glance.

The mark is the Eisenhower Matrix in its four fixed pigments, straight from the GSD Design Reference. A single check sits on the Do First tile, because sorting is only half the promise; finishing is the other half. No letterforms, no gloss, no gradient, and nothing borrowed from the crowd of blue productivity icons.

The tiles follow the shape law exactly: continuous-corner squircles built as true superellipses, never circular corner approximations. The check is a knockout, so it reads as paper on paper, dark on dark, and stays honest in one-color use.

02 · Color

Four pigments carry the meaning.

Each pigment is a quadrant, always in its fixed position, and each adapts between light and dark exactly as the app's tokens do. Every chip below shows both values, light over dark. Tide pulls double duty by design: it is Schedule's pigment in the mark, and elsewhere it is the single interactive accent, reserved for genuine actions and links.

Warm paper on the home screen.

Light ships as the primary; dark uses the dark pigment set on dark paper. The favicon shares the mark's geometry and swaps palettes automatically with the visitor's dark mode.

05 · Typography

A serif for the voice, a sans for the work.

On Apple platforms the product speaks New York, per the design reference. The wordmark ships outlined in Gelasio, the open serif that is metric compatible with Georgia, which is exactly the cross-platform fallback the site's own font stack declares. Brand and site render as one voice on every device, with no license friction.

The subline stays a quiet tracked sans, standing in for SF. Nothing in these files depends on a font being installed.

06 · Usage

Guardrails, briefly.

  • Clear space equals one tile on all sides.
  • Minimum sizes: mark 16 px, compact lockup 22 px tall, full lockup 40 px tall.
  • Below 40 px tall, drop the subline and use the compact lockup or the mark.
  • On photography, place the mark on a paper or dark-paper ground first.
  • The pigments are a fixed vocabulary. Never reorder, recolor, or omit a tile.
  • The check belongs to Do First only, and only one check ever appears.
  • Continuous corners only. Never circular corners, never sharp.
  • Do not set the wordmark in another typeface or add effects to the mark.
