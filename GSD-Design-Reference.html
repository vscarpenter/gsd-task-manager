01 — Philosophy

Three deliberate moves

The app should read as a calm, editorial, native iOS experience — never a generic productivity tool. Everything follows from three principles. When in doubt, defer to them.

01 · Color earns its place

The only strong color in the app is the four quadrant pigments. Chrome, nav icons, charts, and settings stay quiet graphite. Color must mean something — a pigment signals a quadrant, the one functional green signals completion, rust signals destructive. No system-blue floods, no rainbow charts, no second palette.

02 · Type carries hierarchy

Apple New York (ships free with iOS) does the headline and quadrant-title work; SF handles body, labels, metadata, and numerals. The serif speaks one editorial voice across page titles, sheet titles, section heads, and quadrant names. Never set body copy in the serif.

03 · Warm, not clinical

A warm paper neutral replaces flat iOS gray — it reads composed and unhurried, like good stationery, and lets the rationed pigments sit naturally. A cool ramp exists as a one-token-swap alternative, never a second design. Elevation is soft, matte, warm-tinted. No glassmorphism, glow, or heavy shadows.

The single rule. This is a refined-native direction — clearly iOS, just calmer and more editorial. Every choice maps to a SwiftUI primitive and a token; nothing fights the platform.

A serif for the voice, SF for the work

06 — Components

One card system carries the app

A thin 3-pt accent spine signals quadrant; an SF semibold title; metadata in a quiet footnote row; a 28-pt completion disc that fills with the quadrant accent when done. Every state shares this structure across every surface.

Anatomy: spine (3 pt, quadrant accent) · title (.headline) · description (.subheadline, ink-2; ink-3 for links) · tag chips (quadrant wash) · meta row (.footnote, ink-3) · 28-pt disc (hairline-strong ring → fills with accent + check when complete).

Card states

  • Default — spine, title, optional description, tags, meta.
  • Completed — strikethrough title in ink-3, disc filled with the quadrant accent + white check.
  • Subtask progress — 84-pt track + done/total; fill is the accent, or green at 100%.
  • Due / overdue — due-today = tide semibold; overdue = alert + warning glyph.
  • Live timer — pulsing accent dot + tabular HH:MM:SS (suppressed under Reduce Motion).
  • Recurrence — repeat glyph in the meta row.
  • Blocked — whole card at 0.62 opacity + a lock "Blocked by n" badge.

Capture bar & chips

  • Capture bar — pill, surface fill, hairline border, soft shadow, pinned above the list. A live quadrant chip recolors and re-labels as shorthand (!! * #tag) is typed.
  • Tag chips — pill, quadrant wash background, quadrant-accent text; 8-pt padding.
  • Quadrant header — fixed 26-pt icon column + serif title in the pigment + quiet graphite tabular count.
  • Toggles — green (success) when on. Buttons/links — tint only for true actions.

07 — Surfaces

The system in place

Each surface, with its layout intent and the colors that carry meaning. Visual mockups (light + dark, iPhone + iPad) live in the companion files GSD Polish — *.html.

Matrix — the hero

iPhone: capture bar + a vertically scrolling list of quadrant sections (Q1→Q4), each a grouped card of task rows. iPad: a true 2×2 board (Q1 top-left → Q4 bottom-right) in NavigationSplitView with a smart-views sidebar; drag a card across a boundary to reclassify. Serif quadrant titles in the pigment; 32-pt separation; capture bar clears the first card by 12 pt when scrolled.

New Task / Edit

iPhone: a detented sheet (.medium → .large) with a serif title. iPad: the same fields as an inspector column. A 2×2 quadrant picker mirrors the matrix — the selected cell fills with the quadrant wash + a full accent border; the others sit at accent-opacity 0.35. Due-date presets in tint; recurrence and other values read graphite.

Browse & Archive

Browse: grouped smart-view list — graphite chrome, pigment only where a row carries quadrant meaning (Today's Focus = rust target, This Week / Ready to Work = tide, This Week's Wins = ochre, Overdue = alert). Archive: dimmed (0.72), read-only, strikethrough rows with the archived date; swipe Restore (tint) / Delete (rust), both with undo.

Dashboard

Serif numerals for every stat; labels and icons graphite (the Streak flame keeps rust). Completion trend = functional green (completed) + graphite dashed (created). Quadrant donut = the four pigments. Top Tags bars = graphite — a tag is not a quadrant, so it borrows no pigment. Every chart binds to the system; nothing invents a color.

Command Palette & Settings

Palette (⌘K): a calm editorial overlay on a dimmed board — serif "Commands", meaning-color smart-view icons, graphite actions, deeper pop shadow. Settings: chrome goes ink, values graphite, the single tint reserved for true actions, destructive rust, toggles green. No wall of blue.

08 — Gestures & motion

The card is the verb; motion has a job

  • Tap — open the editor (sheet on iPhone, inspector on iPad).
  • Leading swipe → — Complete / Uncomplete. Green fill, .success haptic, confetti on complete. Full-swipe triggers without lifting.
  • Trailing swipe ← — Snooze (slate) and Delete (rust). Delete confirms once.
  • Long-press — lifts the card into the full menu: Edit · Complete · Start/Stop timer · Snooze · Duplicate · Share · Move to quadrant · Delete (rust, last). Same menu on iPad secondary-click.
  • Capture → classify — the chip recolors live as shorthand is typed; on submit the new card drops into its quadrant and focus stays for rapid serial capture.

09 — Empty states & onboarding

Empty, not blank — and a first run that won't nag

Empty-state anatomy

  • Mark — one SF Symbol in ink-3 on a 60-pt sunken tile. Tinted only to reassure (green check for "Nothing overdue"). Never a colorful illustration.
  • Headline — one serif .title3 line, calm and factual.
  • Body — one sentence, ≤ 2 lines, ink-2: what will appear here and why it's empty.
  • Action — at most one, in tint; omit where there's nothing useful to do.
  • In-context empties (a quadrant in a populated matrix) use a dashed prompt row, never a full-screen takeover.

Onboarding — four screens, always skippable

  • Order — Welcome → The Matrix → Capture shorthand → Privacy & sync. Page dots; Skip top-right on every screen.
  • No forced sign-in — the last screen's primary action is Start using GSD (offline); Sign in to sync is the quiet secondary.
  • Re-showable from Settings → About. Reduce Motion cross-fades instead of sliding.
  • Voice — plain, unhurried, second person; the privacy line is literally true about where data lives.

10 — Iconography & app mark

Four tiles, one decision

SF Symbols throughout, consistent stroke weight and optical sizing. The app icon is a 2×2 of the quadrant pigments on warm paper — the product's whole idea in one glance — with a single check on the Do-First tile. No letterforms, no gloss; it reads at every size and stands apart from the sea of blue-gradient productivity icons.
